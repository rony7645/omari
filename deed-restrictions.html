Deed Restrictions

Constitution

CONSTITUTION SHEPHERD PARK TERRACE CIVIC CLUB

ARTICLE I – SECTION 1

The name of this organization shall be Shepherd Park Terrace Civic Club (hereinafter referred to as Club).

ARTICLE II – SECTION 2

The period of its duration shall be perpetual.

ARTICLE III – SECTION 3

The club shall be a non-profit organization for the purpose of advocating and bringing about improvements, to assist in the enforcement of any and all binding restrictions and to advocate civic necessities that will be conducive to the progress of the community and the well-being of its citizens.

ARTICLE IV – SECTION 4

The boundaries of Shepherd Park Terrace Civic Club shall be and hereby are established in accordance with the map and plat records on file in the office of the County Clerk, Houston, Harris County, Texas, concerning Shepherd Park Terrace Subdivision 1, 2 and 3.

ARTICLE V – SECTION 5

Membership in this Club shall be open to all homeowners of Shepherd Park Terrace Subdivisions 1, 2 and 3 residing within the bounds described in Article IV, Section 4. Member household shall pay such dues and assessments and have such rights as are provided in the by-laws of this Club. Each household is entitled to two votes per lot.

ARTICLE VI – SECTION 6

The officers of this Club shall be President, Vice-President, Recording Secretary, Treasurer and Parliamentarian, and all Block Directors. These officers and directors will be elected for a term of two years. The officers and directors together shall be designated as the Board. The Board shall have the management and control of the affairs of this Club; provided, however, that the members shall have the right to initiate resolutions, plans, policies and projects as the by-laws may direct.

ARTICLE VII – SECTION 7

Meetings of the members and Board shall be as prescribed by the by-laws and for all purposes of the Club a quorum shall consist of a majority of the members of the Board when the Board wishes to act, and fifteen (15) members in good standing, at the meetings when the members are to act. A majority vote of the members of the Board or of the Club at which a quorum is present shall be the act of such Board or of the Club.

ARTICLE VIII – SECTION 8

The constitution may be amended from time to time by resolution in writing presented at a regular meeting of the Club, read and approved by a two-thirds vote at two successive regular meetings at which a quorum is present at each meeting...

By-Laws

BY-LAWS OF SHEPHERD PARK TERRACE CIVIC CLUB

ARTICLE I – SECTION 1 (MANAGEMENT – Officers)

The management of the Club shall be vested in the Board of Directors, constituted as provided by and subject to Article VI of the Constitution of the Club.

ARTICLE II – SECTION 2 (MEETINGS / ELECTIONS)

The Club may have regular and special meetings. The Club shall have at least one regular meeting every other month. The Board of Directors shall determine the date of the regular and special meetings. Special meetings of the Club can be called by the President, Vice President or a majority of the Board of Directors at any time other than when the regular meeting is held, by giving due and timely notice thereof. Notice must be sent to the members no less than seven (7) days before the meeting and it must state the place, day, hour and purpose of the meeting. A quorum for a special meeting shall consist of fifteen (15) members in good standing. A member in good standing can vote in person at all regular and special meetings. Any member who is delinquent for sixty (60) days in payment of any dues or special assessments or is in violation of the Covenants, Conditions and Restrictions is not in good standing. It shall be the duty of the president to call a meeting of the full membership of the club upon the written request of fifteen (15) or more members in good standing; such request shall state the objective of the called meeting. Notice of special meetings of the Club under (c) and (d) shall be given by the Recording Secretary not later than one (1) week after the receipt of a written request as provided in (c) and (d) above. The notice shall state the objective, the date and the hour of the meeting. Said meeting shall not be held earlier than fifteen (15) days from the date of the notice. No other business shall be presented for which it is called and so indicated in said notice.

ARTICLE III – SECTION 3 (BOARD MEETINGS)

The Board shall hold at least one meeting every other month, but they may, at their discretion, suspend meetings during June, July, and August.

ARTICLE IV – SECTION 4 (BOUNDARY)

The boundaries of Shepherd Park Terrace Civic Club shall be and hereby are established in accordance with the map and plat records on file in the office of the County Clerk, Houston, Harris County, Texas, concerning Shepherd Park Terrace Subdivision 1, 2 and 3.

ARTICLE V – SECTION 5 (MEMBERSHIP)

Membership in this Club shall be open to all homeowners of Shepherd Park Terrace Subdivision 1, 2 and 3 residing within the bounds described in Article IV. ARTICLE VI – ASSESSMENTS

ARTICLE VI – SECTION 6 (ASSESSMENTS)

Member households shall pay dues and assessments and have such rights as are provided in the bylaws and constitution.

ARTICLE VII – SECTION 7 (BOARD OF DIRECTORS)

The Board of Directors of the Club shall consist of the following officers: President, Vice President, Treasurer, Secretary, Corresponding Secretary, Parliamentarian, and all Block Directors. The regular meeting of the Board of Directors shall be open to all Club members in good standing. Incoming and outgoing officers shall meet ten (10) to fifteen (15) days after the election to set a proposed budget for the ensuing year, which will be presented at the next meeting. Outgoing officers shall turn over all books and papers to the incoming officers at that time. The Board Members will serve a term of two (2) years before their position is up for election again. The Officers of the Board shall be elected by a majority of a quorum of the members who are present in person, not more than thirty (30) days and not less than seven (7) days prior to the annual meeting. The Election meeting of this Club shall be held at 7:30 PM on the third Monday in March every other year. This meeting may be postponed for not more than thirty (30) days at the discretion of the Board. Notice of the annual meeting shall be published by the Secretary of the Club to the members in good standing at least ten (10) days prior to the date of the election. At least ninety (90) days before the election meeting, the President shall appoint a Nomination Committee composed of three members in good standing, one (1) from each section. Nothing in these bylaws shall be construed to prevent nominations from the floor at an Election meeting. Final ballots for voting shall include the names of the Nominating Committee’s choice and any other names of members who wish to hold positions, as long as their names are submitted to the President sixty (60) days prior to the election. DUTIES AND POWER OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS The Board of Directors shall have the right to administer and manage the affairs of the Club. It shall keep a correct record of its proceedings and submit a report of the same to the members at the regular meetings. It shall have the power to authorize expenditures of the Club and hire employees, agents and independent contractors to provide services for the Club. The Board of Directors may require any officer and/or employee of the Club who shall have custody of funds to post a Bond, with a surety or sureties that are satisfactory to the Board. VACANCIES A vacancy in the office of a Director shall occur by (a) resignation in writing filed with the Secretary; (b) absence from three consecutive meetings, whether regular or special; (c) moving from the area; (d) death. If any Director shall fail to attend the regular monthly meeting of the Board for three consecutive meetings without good and valid reasons, and without having given notice of his/her absence, such Director shall automatically cease to be a member of the Board and the vacancy will be filled by approval of a majority of the other Board members. All unexpired terms or vacancies of Directors shall be filled by approval of a majority of the Board of Directors until that position is up for election. The majority of the members in good standing who are present at a meeting called for such purpose may remove any Director of the Association from office with just cause. Any officer of the Club may be removed by approval of the majority of the Board of Directors with justified cause. DUTIES AND/OR POWERS OF THE OFFICERS The President shall have power to preside at all meetings of the Club, appoint all committees not otherwise provided for in the governing document, sign all official documents and countersign checks with the Treasurer, and be an ex-officio member of all committees. In the absence of the duly elected President of the Board, the Vice-President of the Club shall perform the duties of such position and in the absence of both the President and Vice-President, the remaining members of the Board by a majority vote may elect one of their members to perform these duties. It shall be the duty of the Vice-President to assist the President in the performance of his/her duties and discharge all other duties required of him/her. The Secretary is the recording officer of the assembly and custodian of its records, except such as are specifically assigned to others. It shall be the duty of the Secretary, under the direction of the President: (1) to properly record all proceedings of the Club and cause them to be written, including payment of dues, other current receipts, disbursements and financial reports; (2) to record the minutes of the meeting(s); (3) submit such records to the Club at its next regular meeting for approval or correction and deliver such to the President prior to each meeting. He/she shall keep the following books of the Club in such form as may be provided: (1) A register for attendance records, in which each member shall sign, and each visitor shall record his/her name, address and telephone number and be placed into the minutes of the meeting. The Secretary shall cause all annual reports, including his/her own, to be printed and copies shall be made available to all present at the Annual Meeting. The Secretary shall provide a summary of monthly meetings and newsletters. It shall be the duty of the Corresponding Secretary to write all official letters which represent the Club. It shall be the duty of the Treasurer to: (a) receive and safely keep all monies and property of every kind which shall be placed in his/her hand by the Secretary or by the Club, and give proper receipts, and promptly deposit same in a bank designated by and in the name of the Club; (b) disburse or transfer same, or any part thereof upon the vote of the Board of Directors; (c) keep an itemized account of all receipts, disbursements and financial condition; and (d) perform such other duties pertaining to his/her direction of the President or Board of Directors; (e) turn over all books and vouchers to be audited upon request. Prior to the termination of each Fiscal Year, the Treasurer shall prepare a Financial Report for the Club, and cause such a report to be presented at the Annual Meeting. All reports shall be submitted in writing. It shall be the duty of the Parliamentarian to become knowledgeable in parliamentary rules and procedures using Robert’s Rules of Order as a standard, to assist the President or presiding officer and the general membership in conducting the meeting in a proper, legal and impartial manner, and to maintain order if the situation dictates.

ARTICLE VIII – SECTION 8 (FISCAL YEAR)

The Fiscal Year of the Club commences on September 1st and ends on August 31st of the following year.

ARTICLE IX – SECTION 9 (ANNUAL REPORT)

The Budget is submitted by the President at the first meeting of the fiscal year.

ARTICLE X – SECTION 10 (AMENDING THE BY-LAWS)

The By-Laws may be amended from time to time, by resolution in writing, presented at a regular meeting of the Club, read and approved by a two-thirds majority vote at two (2) successive regular meetings at which a quorum is present at each meeting.

ARTICLE XI – SECTION 11

Meetings of Directors, Officers, Board and/or the membership of this Club may be held at such place or places within Harris County, Texas, as may from time to time be designated by the Board.

ARTICLE XII – SECTION 12 (DUES)

The annual assessment for each member household shall be $50.00 and shall be due between February 1st and April 30th each year. The assessment may increase by a majority of the voting members, that is, two-thirds (2/3) present at the meeting.

ARTICLE XIII – SECTION 13

Special assessment against each member may be by a two-thirds vote of the members in good standing present at any meeting of the members at which a quorum, as defined in the Constitution, is present, provided that no such assessment shall exceed the sum of $50.00 per household for any one (1) year, and provided further that such proposal for assessments shall have been submitted in writing at the previous regular meeting of the Club.

ARTICLE XIV – SECTION 14 (COMMITTEES)

There shall be, in addition to such committees as the Board shall determine, the following standing committees, with such duties as the Board may designate: • Architectural Control • Membership Committee • Beautification Committee • Program Committee • Governmental Liaison Committee • Recreational Activities Committee • Law Enforcement and Safety Committee • Sanitation Committee • Grievance Committee The Chairperson of such committee shall be appointed by the President with the approval of the Board, and the Chairperson of such committee may select three (3) or more members to serve on his/her committee, at least one (1) of whom shall be selected from each section.

ARTICLE XV – SECTION 16 (ORDER OF BUSINESS)

1. Reading of Minutes of the Last Meeting
2. Treasurer’s Report
3. Committee Reports
4. Unfinished Business
5. New Business
6. Program or Miscellaneous
7. Notification of Next Regular Meeting
8. Adjournment

SECTION 17 (ROBERT'S RULES)

Robert’s Rules of Order shall determine the conduct of business in all meetings of the Club, its governmental bodies and committees, except where inconsistent with the Constitution and these by-laws.

Amendment of Shepherd Park Terrace Subdivision Restrictions

Definitions (Terms & Roles)
  • Board: The Board of Directors of Shepherd Park Terrace Civic Club, governing body.
  • Owner: Legal title holder of any portion of the Property, including heirs, executors, assigns.
  • Vote: Each member household gets up to 2 votes if both names appear on the deed.
  • Amendment: The amendment to Shepherd Park Terrace Subdivision Restrictions.
  • Property: The real property described, including future additions under committee jurisdiction.
  • Lot: Any lot described in the subdivision restrictions, including building sites from resubdivision/consolidation.
  • Developed Lot: Lot with improvements and utilities installed.
  • Undeveloped Lot: Lot without improvements/utilities.
  • Perpetual: Lasting forever.
  • Single-family: Household groups defined by blood, marriage, or agreed unrelated persons.
Garage Location Rules

All newly constructed residences shall have detached garages at the rear. No attached garages except corner lots with a breezeway.

Building Street Frontage

Buildings shall be constructed to face the street of the lot; corner lots face the street with smallest frontage.

Temporary Residence / Additions

No trailer, camper, RV, tent, shack, garage, or outbuilding may be used as residence. Storage only in garage or out of public view.

Business / Commercial Restrictions

All lots for single-family residential use only. No commercial or professional activities disturbing residential character.

Minimum Setback Rules

Front: 25 ft, Side: 10 ft, Interior: 5 ft. Detached garages: 3 ft from interior lot line.

Leasing Restrictions

No home may be leased under local, state, or federal housing subsidy programs. No subleasing.

Minimum Sq Ft & Exterior Materials

One-and-half-story: ≥1400 sq ft, One-story: ≥1800 sq ft. Exterior: ≥51% masonry. Roof: composition shingles.

Easements

Reserved as shown on recorded plat for utility installation and maintenance.

Fences & Walls

No fence, wall, hedge, pergola, or detached structure forward of front building line without approval of Architectural Control Committee.

Additions & Modifications

Any addition or alteration must be submitted in writing and approved by Architectural Control Committee before construction.

Exterior Maintenance & Appearance

Clothes lines, window AC units, satellite dishes not allowed in front/side yard. Maintain lot and improvements, landscaping visible from street.

Trash, Signs, Parking & Repairs

No dumping or burning. Signs limited to real estate / small warnings. Vehicles parked in driveway/garage; major repairs not visible.

Pets, Noise & Illegal Activities

Only common pets allowed; leash laws apply. No loud/offensive noise. No sale of alcohol/controlled substances. No drilling/mining.

Subdivision & Enforcement

Lots cannot be subdivided. Parcels of more than two lots may be re-subdivided, following setbacks/frontage. Invalid provisions do not affect remaining restrictions.
